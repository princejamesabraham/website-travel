Lawang Sewu
Since | 20 Century History of Lawang Sewu

Lawang Sewu Semarang is a historical building that used to function as the headquarters of the Dutch East Indies railway company, Nederlandsch-Indische Spoorweg-Maatschappij (NIS). This building was built in the early 20th century, around 1903-1918, and has been a silent witness to various historical events, including the Japanese occupation and the Indonesian struggle for independence.

kota lama
Since | 17 Century History of Kota lama

Semarang's Old Town has a long history, especially since the 17th century when it was the center of trade and Dutch colonial government. This area was a silent witness to the agreement between the Islamic Mataram Kingdom and the VOC, which wanted to control Semarang as an important port.

Pondok Kopi
Since | 1915 History of Pondok Kopi

Pondok Kopi in Semarang, especially Dharma Boutique Roastery (formerly Margo Redjo), was founded in 1915 by Tan Tiong Ie. The main advantage of Pondok Kopi lies in its strategic location. From this place, visitors can look in all directions and witness a row of mountains standing majestically. There are Mount Merbabu, Telomoyo, and Mount Andong.

Tugu Muda
Since | 1953 History of Tugu Muda

Tugu Muda in Semarang was built to commemorate the Five Day Battle that took place on 14-19 October 1945. This battle was an important moment in the history of Indonesia's struggle for independence against the remnants of Japanese troops in Semarang after the Proclamation. Tugu Muda is a monument in the form of a candle, symbolizing the spirit of struggle that never dies.

Sampo Kong
Since | 17 century History of Sam poo Kong

Sam Poo Kong Temple is a temple that has a long history and is closely related to the arrival of Admiral Cheng Ho in Semarang in the 15th century. This temple was built on a location believed to be a resting place and treatment for his helmsman, Wang Jing Hong, who was sick when Cheng Ho's fleet anchored at Simongan Beach.

Dusun Semilir
May 25 | 2019 Recreational Tourism

Located in Bawen District, Semarang Regency, this destination began construction in 2018 and was officially opened to the public in 2019. From the start, Dusun Semilir was designed as a thematic tourism area that highlights cultural values ​​and the beauty of typical Javanese architecture.

Saloka Theme Park
June 22 | 2019 Recreational Tourism

SALOKA is present as one of the tourist destinations of Pesona Indonesia in the form of a family thematic recreation park in Central Java that carries the concept of local wisdom. Located at the intersection of the cities of Semarang, Salatiga, Surakarta and the Special Region of Yogyakarta.

Gedong Songo Temple
Since | 8 century History of Gedong Songo Temple

Gedong Songo Temple is a Hindu temple complex located on the slopes of Mount Ungaran, Semarang, Central Java. This building is estimated to have been built in the 8th century AD, during the reign of the Ancient Mataram Kingdom, Sanjaya Dynasty. This temple was rediscovered by Loten in 1740 and was named Gedong Pitoe by Thomas Raffles in 1804 because there were seven temples.
